Baghdad

Baghdad is the capital and largest city of Iraq, located on the Tigris River. Founded in 762 CE, it became a major center of learning and culture during the Islamic Golden Age, and was once the largest city in the world. Today, it remains Iraq's economic and cultural hub and the second-largest city in the Arab world.

Mosul

Mosul is Iraq's second-largest city, a historic cultural hub located on the Tigris River, which was known as Nineveh in ancient times. It's a strategic crossroads with a diverse population and rich history, though it suffered extensive damage during the ISIS occupation from 2014-2017, which is now being addressed through ongoing reconstruction efforts.

Basra

Basra is a major port city in southern Iraq, founded in 636 AD at the junction of the Tigris and Euphrates rivers, forming the Shatt al-Arab. Historically known as the "Venice of the East" for its canals, it was a significant trading hub during the Islamic Golden Age and the seat of powerful cultural and scientific advancements. Today, it is Iraq's main port, a center for the country's oil industry, and one of the hottest cities in Iraq.
